# Go-To-Market Strategy — SoftSell

## Target Customer

### Primary: IT Managers at Mid-Size Companies (50–500 employees)
- Pain: Unused licenses from team changes, layoffs, project pivots
- Goal: Recover budget, simplify software asset management
- Where they are: LinkedIn, Reddit (r/sysadmin), IT forums, G2

### Secondary: Startups & Scale-ups Buying Software
- Pain: Need professional tools but can't afford retail pricing
- Goal: Access verified software at startup-friendly prices
- Where they are: Product Hunt, Hacker News, startup Slack communities

---

## Phase 1 — Launch (Month 1–2)

**Goal:** First 20 seller transactions, validate the model

### Channels
- **Cold outreach to IT managers on LinkedIn** — target companies that recently announced layoffs or restructuring (they have the most unused licenses)
- **Post on r/sysadmin and r/msp** — these communities actively discuss software asset management
- **Product Hunt launch** — drives early adopter buyers and press attention
- **Founder's personal network** — direct outreach to 50 warm contacts in enterprise IT

### Messaging
Lead with the seller side: "Get paid for licenses you're not using." Don't lead with the buyer value prop yet — supply needs to come before demand.

---

## Phase 2 — Growth (Month 3–6)

**Goal:** $50K GMV/month, build SEO moat

### Channels
- **SEO content** — "How to sell unused Adobe licenses", "Microsoft license resale guide" — high intent, low competition keywords
- **Partnership with IT asset management tools** — integrate with tools like Snow Software, Flexera
- **Referral program** — sellers who refer other sellers get a reduced commission on their next deal
- **Case studies** — publish 3 detailed case studies showing real ROI for sellers

---

## Phase 3 — Scale (Month 7–12)

**Goal:** $500K GMV/month, category leadership

### Channels
- **Google Ads** — target "sell software license" and "buy cheap software" keywords
- **Enterprise sales motion** — dedicated account managers for companies with $50K+ in licenses
- **Press** — pitch to TechCrunch, VentureBeat on the "software waste" narrative
