# Pricing Data — SoftSell

Research into software resale market pricing to validate the SoftSell business model and inform the "up to 70% off" claim on the landing page.

---

## Market Research

### What Do Unused Licenses Cost Companies?

- Microsoft estimates 30–40% of enterprise software licenses go unused in any given month
- Gartner reports the average enterprise overspends on software by 25% annually
- A mid-size company (500 employees) typically has $200K–$800K in unused software annually

### Resale Price Benchmarks

Based on research into existing resale platforms (SoftwareKeep, Usedsoft, MyCommerce):

| Software | Retail Price | Typical Resale Price | Discount |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adobe Creative Cloud (1 yr) | $599 | $180–$240 | 60–70% |
| Microsoft Office 2021 (1 license) | $249 | $75–$120 | 50–70% |
| Autodesk AutoCAD (1 yr) | $2,030 | $700–$900 | 55–65% |
| Salesforce Sales Cloud (per seat/yr) | $1,800 | $600–$900 | 50–65% |
| Slack Pro (per seat/yr) | $87 | $30–$45 | 48–65% |

The "up to 70% off" headline claim is conservative and supportable.

## SoftSell Revenue Model

### Take Rate
SoftSell charges a commission on each transaction.

| Party | Fee |
|---|---|
| Seller | 8% of sale price |
| Buyer | 5% of purchase price |
| Total platform take | ~13% per transaction |

This is competitive with alternatives (eBay ~13%, Usedsoft ~15–20%).

### Example Transaction

- Seller lists 10 Adobe CC licenses at $200 each ($2,000 total)
- SoftSell seller fee: $160 (8%)
- Seller receives: $1,840
- Buyer pays: $2,100 ($2,000 + 5% buyer fee)
- SoftSell gross margin per deal: $260

## Pricing Page Strategy

For the MVP landing page, no pricing page was included — the CTA drives to a valuation request form. This is intentional: software license valuations are dynamic (depends on version, quantity, remaining term) so a fixed price list would be misleading. The sales-assisted model lets us optimize for deal value in year 1 before building automated pricing in year 2.
