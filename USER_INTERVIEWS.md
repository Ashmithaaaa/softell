# User Interviews — SoftSell

Simulated user research based on the target personas. In a real launch these would be conducted with actual IT managers and startup founders before building.

---

## Interview 1 — IT Manager, 200-person SaaS Company

**Role:** IT Manager  
**Company size:** 200 employees  
**Context:** Company went through a 15% layoff 6 months ago

**Q: What happens to software licenses when someone leaves?**
"Honestly, most of the time nothing. The account gets deactivated but the license just sits there. We have a spreadsheet but it's always out of date. I'd estimate we have 30–40 Adobe and Microsoft licenses we're not using."

**Q: Have you tried to sell them before?**
"I looked into it once. Found some sketchy-looking sites but didn't trust them with our license keys. Gave up after 20 minutes."

**Q: What would make you trust a platform enough to use it?**
"Knowing someone had actually verified the buyer. And getting paid fast — I don't want to chase a payment for 6 weeks."

**Q: What's the biggest friction point?**
"Time. I have 50 other things to do. If it's not simple, I'm not doing it."

**Key insight:** Trust and speed are the primary barriers. The landing page correctly leads with "48hr payout" and "verified licenses."

## Interview 2 — Founder, 8-person Startup

**Role:** Founder/CEO  
**Company size:** 8 employees  
**Context:** Just raised a seed round, building out the team

**Q: How do you currently source software for the team?**
"Mostly just buy retail or use free tiers. We needed Figma for the design team and that was fine. But Autodesk for our one engineer who needs CAD tools — that's $2K a year. It hurts."

**Q: Would you buy a second-hand license if you could?**
"If I knew it was legit, absolutely. I don't care if someone else used it before me, I just need it to work."

**Q: What would stop you?**
"Fear of it not being real, getting locked out, or the original owner reclaiming it. I need a guarantee."

**Q: What's the max discount that would feel too good to be true?**
"Like... 80%+ would make me nervous. 60–70% feels believable."

**Key insight:** Buyer anxiety is about legitimacy and reclamation risk. The 30-day money-back guarantee and "verified licenses" messaging directly address this. The 70% figure in the hero is at the believable ceiling.

## Interview 3 — CFO, 400-person Enterprise

**Role:** CFO  
**Company size:** 400 employees  
**Context:** Running a software audit to cut costs

**Q: How much do you think you spend on unused software annually?**
"We just did an audit. It's embarrassing — about $340K in licenses we're either not using or heavily underusing. Our board asked about it directly last quarter."

**Q: Who would handle selling those licenses?**
"IT manager would do the work but I'd need to approve anything over $10K. And legal would want to review the resale terms for our enterprise agreements."

**Q: What's the ideal outcome?**
"Recover $100–150K, simplify the stack, and have documentation that we handled it properly for compliance purposes."

**Key insight:** Enterprise deals are large but have more stakeholders. The contact form correctly captures "company" to let the sales team identify high-value leads and route them to a white-glove process.

## Summary of Findings

| Theme | Frequency | Landing Page Response |
|---|---|---|
| Trust / verification anxiety | 3/3 | "Verified & Secure" in Why Us |
| Speed of payout | 2/3 | "48hr" stat in hero |
| Process simplicity | 2/3 | 3-step How It Works |
| Price believability | 2/3 | "Up to 70%" not "up to 90%" |
| Compliance / documentation | 1/3 | Future feature — not on MVP |
