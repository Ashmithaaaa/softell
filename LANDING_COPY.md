# Landing Copy — SoftSell

All copy used on the landing page, with rationale for each decision.

---

## Hero Section

**Headline:**
> Turn Unused Software Into Real Cash

**Rationale:** Led with the seller value prop because supply is the harder side of a marketplace. "Real Cash" is more visceral than "revenue" or "value." Short, punchy, no jargon.

**Alternatives considered:**
- "The Marketplace for Software License Resale" — too category-descriptive, not benefit-led
- "Stop Wasting Money on Software You Don't Use" — too negative, loss-framing upfront can feel accusatory
- "Sell Your Unused Software Licenses" — too literal, lacks energy

**Subheadline:**
> SoftSell is the marketplace where businesses sell unused software licenses and buyers get verified software at up to 70% off retail price.

**Rationale:** Covers both sides of the marketplace in one sentence. "Verified" is doing important trust work. "70% off retail" gives a concrete anchor.

**Primary CTA:** Sell My Licenses  
**Secondary CTA:** See How It Works →

**Rationale:** Primary CTA is seller-focused (supply-first strategy). Arrow on secondary CTA signals "scroll/learn more" without leaving the page.

## Testimonials

Testimonials were written to represent three different buyer/seller personas:

1. **Sarah Mitchell (IT Manager)** — Seller story. Volume deal (40 licenses). Emphasizes speed ("two days").
2. **David Okafor (Startup Founder)** — Buyer story. Emphasizes price savings ("65% off") and ease.
3. **Priya Nair (CFO)** — Repeat seller. Emphasizes trust and improving experience over time.

Each testimonial mentions a specific number (40 licenses, 65% off, 3 rounds) — specificity makes testimonials feel real.

## Contact Form

**Headline:** Ready to Sell?  
**Subheadline:** Tell us about your licenses and we'll get back with a valuation within 24 hours.

**Fields:**
- Full Name (required)
- Work Email (required) — "Work" signals B2B positioning
- Company (optional) — lets us identify enterprise leads
- License Type (required dropdown) — qualifies the lead
- Message (optional) — open field for context

**CTA Button:** Get My Valuation →

**Rationale:** "Get My Valuation" is more specific than "Submit" or "Send." It tells the user exactly what happens next.

## Copy Principles Used

1. **Benefit-led, not feature-led** — "Get Paid Fast" not "Fast Payment Processing"
2. **Specific numbers build trust** — "48hr" not "quickly", "70%" not "significant savings"
3. **Active voice throughout** — "Get a Valuation" not "A Valuation Will Be Provided"
4. **No jargon** — "unused software" not "underutilized software assets"
5. **Seller-first framing** — supply is the harder marketplace problem
